# TorBot — My Role

## 1. Purpose

This document is a straightforward account of my role on TorBot: what I designed, built, and operated. It exists so readers of this showcase understand the scope of ownership behind the work.

## 2. My Role

I led the design, architecture, implementation, and evolution of TorBot. The decisions documented across this repository are mine, as is responsibility for the trade-offs behind them.

## 3. What I Owned

- **Product** — defining the problem, the scope, and what TorBot deliberately does not do.
- **Architecture** — the separation into conversation, orchestration, data, and visibility, and the multi-tenant approach.
- **Implementation** — the booking logic, the conversational flow, the data layer, and the dashboard.
- **Operation** — keeping the system running and reliable across multiple businesses.

## 4. Tools & Collaboration

I used modern tooling, including AI-assisted development and automation, as part of my workflow. These were tools in service of my engineering decisions — useful for speed and iteration — not a substitute for the design judgment, trade-off analysis, and ownership described here. The architecture and the decisions behind it are my own.

## 5. Skills Demonstrated

- System design and clear separation of concerns.
- Multi-tenant thinking on shared infrastructure.
- Integration with external services as boundaries.
- Conversation-driven product design.
- Operational and reliability awareness.

## 6. Learning & Iteration

TorBot improved through iteration as much as up-front design. Specific lessons are covered in [`lessons-learned.md`](./lessons-learned.md).

## 7. What Is Intentionally Not Documented

This document does not name employers, clients, customers, or any third parties, and it does not describe internal implementation. It accounts for my role, not the production system.
