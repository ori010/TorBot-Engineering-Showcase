# TorBot — Why TorBot Exists

## 1. The Problem

Service businesses lose bookings to friction. Customers call when the business is busy, send messages that get missed, or give up when asked to install an app or fill in a web form. On the business side, manually reconciling calls, messages, and a calendar leads to double-booked slots and no-shows that go untracked.

## 2. Why Existing Tools Fall Short

Most booking tools assume the customer will come to them — open an app, create an account, or use a web form. That assumption adds a step exactly where bookings are most likely to be abandoned. The channel customers already use for everyday conversation is rarely treated as a real booking interface.

## 3. The Core Insight

The lowest-friction booking is one that happens where the customer already is. For a large share of service-business customers, that place is WhatsApp. Treating WhatsApp as a first-class booking interface — not just a notification channel — removes the single biggest point of drop-off.

## 4. Why WhatsApp-First Made Sense

- It is already installed and used daily by the target customers.
- It supports a natural, conversational booking flow with no new login.
- It keeps the whole interaction — booking, rescheduling, cancellation — in one familiar thread.
- It lets a business offer a modern booking experience without asking customers to change behavior.

## 5. Who It's For

TorBot is built for small and mid-sized service businesses — salons, clinics, studios, and similar — that book appointments and already communicate with customers over messaging.

## 6. What "Good" Looks Like

- **For the customer:** book, reschedule, or cancel in a few messages, with no app and no waiting on hold.
- **For the business:** fewer missed bookings, no double-booking, and a single clear view of what's happening.

## 7. Non-Goals

TorBot is not a general calendar tool, a CRM, a marketing platform, or a payments system. Its scope is the booking lifecycle and the visibility a business needs to operate it. Staying narrow is deliberate — it keeps the product focused on removing booking friction.
